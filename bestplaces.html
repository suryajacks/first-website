    • Must visiting spots!

    1. Taj mahal,Agra

      2. Agra is home to one of the world’s seven wonders, the Taj Mahal.
      The Taj Mahal is also the most well-known testament to the power of love in the world.
      Mumtaz Mahal, Emperor Shah Jahan’s wife, was the inspiration for the name.
      The best time to visit is either at dawn or dusk, when the lighting
      transforms the atmosphere dramatically. It is one of the top tourist places in India.

    2. Golden Temple,Amristar

      3. Amritsar is a significant historical and cultural centre for Sikhs.
      Harmandir Sahib, also known as the Golden Temple because of its ornate gold decorations,
      is the main attraction of Amritsar. On the site of the temple, there is a man-made pool
      that was completed by Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru. The temple is open 24 hours a day,
      seven days a week. The spiritual atmosphere of the temple always impresses visitors,
      which is heightened by the prayers chanted from the Sikh holy book and
      played throughout the temple. Every visitor is entitled to free meals, known as ‘langar.’

    3. Charminar,Hyderabad

      4. Hyderabad, also known as the City of the Nawabs, has its own distinct ancient charm.
      Old mosques and bazaars can be found in Hyderabad. The most distinct monument of the city,
      Charminar, is an Urdu word that means “four minars or pillars.” It is a cultural and historical landmark.
      Charminar is known for its elaborate architectural history,
      making it one of India’s most well-known structures. Locals and tourists,
      alike are familiar with the surrounding Laal Bazaar and the 400-year-old mosque
      on the top floor of Charminar. Secret: It is said there is an underground tunnel
      that connects the Golconda Fort to Charminar.

    4. The Red Fort,New Delhi

      5. The capital city of India, New Delhi, is home to the Indian government,
      the Rashtrapati Bhavan, Parliament House, and the Supreme Court of India,
      as well as several historical sites. Out of all the tourist attractions in New Delhi,
      the Red Fort is the most popular.The splendid crescent-shaped
      Red Fort was built by Shah Jahan. It is named after the magnificent red sandstone
      used in its build. The Lahore Gate (the fort’s main entrance) and the Delhi Gate are
      the fort’s two gates. Each evening, the visitors are treated to a sound and light show
      depicting significant events in the fort’s history.

    5. Goa Beaches

      6. For those looking for a great beach vacation, Goa has long been a “go-to” destination.
      Goa is the pinnacle of Indian tourism, with some of the best beaches, cuisine, and nightlife.
      Agonda Beach is a good choice for those seeking peace and quiet,
      while Calangute Beach is the most mainstreamed and crowded.
      The beaches of Mandrem, Morjim, and Ashwem are ideal for those looking for
      upscale resorts, yoga retreats, and spa vacations. Throughout the year, millions of visitors
      from both within and outside the country flock to this location. So yes,
      Goa is one of the top tourist places in India.
